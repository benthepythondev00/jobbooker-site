HVAC speed-to-lead: winning the no-cool call in a heat wave

Emergency work has no queue

When an AC unit dies in a Houston or Dallas heat wave, the homeowner does not shortlist and deliberate — they fire off two or three enquiries and book whoever answers first with a credible next step. "We'll get back to you tomorrow" is functionally a decline.

Invoca's July 2026 home-services benchmarks show how fragile inbound contact already is: only 52% of callers reached a person, and even calls lasting over 15 seconds were answered just 65% of the time. Web forms have no ringing phone; they wait silently while the customer books a competitor.

Peak season is exactly when intake breaks

The cruel arithmetic of HVAC: the weeks with the most valuable inbound demand are the weeks every technician is booked solid and nobody watches the inbox. A historical InsideSales.com result — contact odds roughly 100× higher at five minutes versus thirty — is phone-era data, but the direction has held everywhere it has been retested.

Know your real number

Pull your last ten web leads and compute time-to-first-substantive-reply for each, including the ones that arrived at 8pm on a Saturday. The average usually surprises owners. JobBooker's browser-only audit computes it locally — nothing is uploaded.

The fix is operational, not heroic

What works is a system that answers every connected lead in under a minute, triages emergency versus scheduleable work with your wording, escalates gas and carbon-monoxide hazards to a human immediately, books real slots from live calendar availability, and reports outcomes you can audit. That is the JobBooker HVAC pilot: $99 first month, then $500 per month, month-to-month.
