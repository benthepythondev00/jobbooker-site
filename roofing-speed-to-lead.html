Roofing speed-to-lead: what slow follow-up actually costs

The maths of an unanswered roofing lead

A Q1 2026 SearchLight sample of ten contractors put the average roofing Google LSA lead at $79. That is the cost of the phone ringing or the form arriving — before anyone has answered it. When a storm-week enquiry sits for three hours because every crew is on a roof, the spend is gone and the job usually is too.

The homeowner's behaviour is predictable: they submit two or three quote requests and book with the first company that responds with something useful. Invoca's July 2026 home-services benchmarks found only 52% of inbound callers even reached a person — web forms, which have no ringing phone to signal urgency, fare worse.

The five-minute cliff

The most-cited response-time study in sales is InsideSales.com's Lead Response Report. One scoped historical result: the odds of making contact were roughly 100× higher at five minutes than at thirty. The study is old and phone-focused, so treat the exact multiple with care — but the direction has been reproduced everywhere: leads decay in minutes, not days.

For a 1–10 truck roofing company the implication is uncomfortable: the window in which a web lead is winnable is usually shorter than a single roof inspection.

Measure it before you fix it

Most owners believe their follow-up is "pretty fast" because they answer when they see the message. The honest number is the time from the lead's timestamp to the first substantive reply — including nights, weekends, and storm weeks. Export your last ten leads and compute it. If you cannot compute it, that is itself the finding.
