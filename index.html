Straight answers

Before you request a pilot

Does JobBooker answer voice calls today?

No. Full inbound voice is planned, not part of the current public pilot. The backend includes signed Twilio webhook and consent controls, but messaging stays inactive until a pilot has approved registration and credentials.

Does it integrate natively with Jobber or Housecall Pro?

No native app is advertised. Google Calendar is the current booking adapter. A custom CRM pilot would require API access, written scope, and—in Jobber’s case—compliance with its app review rules.

What does “under 60 seconds” mean?

It is the pilot’s operational target from JobBooker receipt to a queued acknowledgement on an activated, consensual channel. The report uses actual provider delivery timestamps, and failures remain visible. It is not a universal internet-to-customer guarantee.

Will the AI promise an emergency arrival time?

No. Hazardous conditions trigger the client-approved safety response and human escalation. JobBooker does not make an unconditional “within an hour” promise.

What happens if Calendar is down or a slot is taken?

The booking stays failed or escalated. The lead is told the time was not confirmed, and the owner gets the failure path. “Booked” is reserved for a successful calendar event.

Do you need my raw leads for the audit?

No. The website calculates sanitized rows locally. You submit only aggregate counts and response timing. If you provide no rows, the result is clearly labelled an industry benchmark.
